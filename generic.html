Professional Biography

Early Life and Education
Collins Kipchirchir Rono was born and raised in Eldama Ravine, Baringo County, Kenya. His academic journey began at Noiwet Primary School, where he demonstrated strong academic potential and a disciplined work ethic. In 2014, he sat for the Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) and scored 359 marks, equivalent to a B+, earning him a spot in a reputable national high school.

In 2015, Collins joined Kabianga High School, one of Kenya’s top-performing schools, for his secondary education. At Kabianga, he not only focused on academics but also nurtured leadership and teamwork skills. He was actively involved in the Christian Union (CU) and developed a passion for mentorship and community service. In 2018, he sat for his Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) and attained a B- (Minus), qualifying him for university education.

University Life – Growth in Leadership and Service
In 2019, Collins joined South Eastern Kenya University (SEKU) to pursue a Bachelor’s degree in Economics and Statistics. University life presented a platform for him to refine his leadership skills, expand his knowledge, and make meaningful contributions to his peers and the community.

During his time at SEKU, Collins took on various leadership and mentorship roles:
Chairman – Christian Union (2021–2022): Collins was elected Chairman of the South Eastern Kenya University Christian Union (SEKU CU), where he successfully doubled the membership from 75 to 150 students. His leadership ensured spiritual nourishment, inclusivity, and unity within the fellowship. As part of his role, he attended the Regional Student Executive Conference (RSEC) in Nairobi, an event organized by FOCUS Kenya. There, he received mentorship from senior Christian leaders on leadership, campus ministry, and life beyond university.

Peer Educator and Counselor (2022–2023): Collins was an active peer counselor, helping students navigate mental health challenges, academic stress, and personal struggles. Through his efforts, over 20 students found a safe space to open up and seek guidance, reinforcing the importance of mental wellness on campus.

Academically, he remained committed to his coursework, excelling in Economics, Statistics, and Data Analysis, and eventually graduating in October 2023 with a Second Class Upper Division.

Building Technical Skills and Work Experience
After completing university, Collins sought to enhance his technical skills and gain practical experience. He enrolled in:

World Best Friend ICT Centre (October – November 2023): He undertook training in computer maintenance, repair, graphic design, and video editing, gaining valuable technical expertise.

ALX Data Science Program (March 2024 – Present): Collins enrolled in ALX’s Data Science program, where he developed proficiency in SQL, Python, Power BI, and advanced Excel for data analytics.

More Than Conquerors School of Theology (April – August 2024): Pursuing his passion for faith and mentorship, Collins enrolled in an Associate of Practical Theology program to strengthen his biblical knowledge and leadership skills.

Professional Experience – Making an Impact in Finance
Loans Officer – Boresha SACCO Society Limited (May – July 2022): Collins secured an internship at Boresha SACCO, a leading microfinance institution. His role included:
- Processing over 30 loan applications daily, ensuring timely disbursement.
- Enhancing customer service, providing prompt responses to inquiries.
- Gaining firsthand experience in credit assessment and risk management.

Relationship Officer – Marble Capital Solutions (September – November 2024): Collins later joined Marble Capital Solutions, a microfinance company, where he:
- Strengthened due diligence processes, integrating site inspections and reducing loan default rates by 30%.
- Conducted background checks, collateral verification, and client visits, improving loan security.

Mentorship and Community Initiatives
Beyond his career, Collins is deeply passionate about mentorship, leadership, and community transformation. Recognizing the challenges faced by men in today’s society, he founded:

Men in Black Accountability Group
A support group for men focusing on:
- Career growth and professional development
- Spiritual and emotional well-being
- Physical fitness and health

The group currently has four committed members who share challenges, support each other, and grow together. Collins envisions expanding this initiative to reach millions of men worldwide, equipping them with guidance on navigating life’s hurdles through mentorship and intentional discussions.

He is also passionate about helping young boys transition into responsible and successful men, providing them with mentorship and role models to guide them through life’s challenges.

Vision for the Future – Making Africa Great
Collins firmly believes that excellence is the key to transforming Africa. His vision is to:
- Use data-driven insights to influence economic development and infrastructure projects in Africa.
- Empower millions of men through mentorship, leadership training, and emotional support.
- Create impactful programs that support young men in career development, mental health, and financial literacy.

With resilience, faith, and commitment, Collins continues to refine his skills, grow his network, and contribute to the betterment of individuals and society. His journey is only beginning, and he is determined to make a lasting impact in Africa and beyond.
